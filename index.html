  • The reference to books on gravestones is altogether a marginal phenomenon of Jewish sepulchral culture;
  • Especially in Hamburg (under Danish rule a place of numerous printing houses), but also in the traditional centres of scholarship such as Worms, Frankfurt or Krakow, the deceased are also remembered by means of their books - even the unwritten ones;
  • The importance of the book is evident from the fact that even the relationship with authors of learned treatises and books is mentioned.

footprints

These books, book titles and authors mentioned on headstones meet the definition of a footprint. In the book studies project of the same name, which traces Jewish Books through time and space, footprints are defined as follows:

A footprint is a record of the material presence of a literary work at a particular time and place

headstones as footprints

  • Books on headstones are footprints:
  • testimony to the material presence of a literary work in a particular time, in a particular place.
